Most people fail on YouTube because they think its a game of always getting more views on every video you make.

Yet when I analysed 40 world-class channels, only 3 in 10 videos performed above their average.

That's just how YouTube works, even when you're doing everything right.
The creators who grow aren't the ones who crack the formula on every video.

They're the ones who look at the 3 that worked and ask why.
- What was the title?
- What was the thumbnail?
- Was it a variation of the problem that landed differently?

Most people are trying to avoid failure.

The ones making progress are mining it for information.
