I got 500K+ views on a B2B YouTube video using this content-gap strategy.

Let's break it down. 👇🏻

Most B2B brands create videos for what they want to say.
Not what people are actually looking for.

That's why their channels stay stuck.

Here's what I did differently:


Step 1: Find where demand > supply
A content gap = high demand + weak competition

Here's how to find one:
• Search topics your ICP cares about
• Check the comments - are people still asking questions?
• Watch the top videos - are they outdated? Fluffy? 10+ mins without answers?

If the existing videos don't deliver, but the view counts are high…
That's a potential gap.


Step 2: Research 20–30 channels

Track their top performers.

Ask:
• Which titles get 10x the views?
• Which title formats consistently hit?
• Which thumbnail styles keep showing up?

Build a swipe file of proven concepts across niches - not just your own.


Step 3: Fill the gap

Once I saw the demand, I acted fast:

• Used a thumbnail that showed result, not topic
• Made a 2-minute video that answered the exact question
• Delivered value fast - no long intro, no fluff, no wasted time


Most businesses guess what to post.
The top ones research what works - then execute.
